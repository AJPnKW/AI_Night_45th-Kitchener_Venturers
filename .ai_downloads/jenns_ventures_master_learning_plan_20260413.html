Rationalized interpretation of your intent:
The night was never meant to begin as only a prompt-editing workbook. It was meant to begin with a human-friendly explanation of AI and ChatGPT, then show why prompt quality matters, then let learners practise in a controlled way, then let them branch into meaningful personal use.

Master learning outcomes

OutcomeWhat learners should leave with
AI understandingA practical explanation of what AI is, what ChatGPT is, and how it differs from “normal software.”
VocabularyComfort with key terms: AI, model/engine, tool, agent, prompt, output, hallucination, bias, verification. Add drift lightly as a facilitator term because it matches your stated intent in this thread.
Prompt skillExperience seeing a weak prompt, improving it, and noticing what changed in the output.
Critical thinkingUnderstanding that helpful output is not automatically correct output.
Personal relevanceOne practical or creative result they care about and one improved prompt they want to keep.

Master session structure

Time Block Instructor goal Participant experience Key concepts Output / artifact
0–10 Arrival, framing, opening prompt Lower pressure, normalize mixed comfort levels, explain the night. Get settled, open browser, write: “One thing I hope AI could help me do better by the end of tonight.” AI is already in daily life; this night is about using it intentionally. Opening note in learner workbook / worksheet.
10–20 AI and ChatGPT foundations Restore the concept layer missing from the simplified workbook version. Hear short plain-language explanation with real examples. AI, ChatGPT, model/engine, tool, agent, normal software vs learned patterns. Shared mental model.
20–28 How ChatGPT works and why prompts matter Make “better prompts” understandable instead of magical. See weak prompt versus stronger prompt live. Prediction, pattern-based output, role / audience / goal / format / constraints. First prompt comparison.
28–40 Guided text-prompt practice Give every learner one successful improvement loop early. Use Guided / Build / Remix starter cards to improve a prompt. Specificity, usefulness, expectation vs result. Improved text prompt + short note on what changed.
40–45 Break / regroup Reset room and devices. Stretch, recharge, reopen tabs if needed. None Room reset.
45–53 Image prompt demo Show that prompt improvement is not only for text. See weak vs stronger image prompt. Subject, mood, style, colour, composition. Image prompt comparison.
53–63 Guided image activity Keep the second hands-on block creative and controlled. Create one image with guided scaffolding. Prompt steering, iteration, clear description. One learner image + short reflection.
63–70 Short advanced/future-path demo Briefly show that AI tools can go further, without shifting the whole night into coding. Observe short facilitator-only Codex / advanced-agent style example. Agent behaviour, structured requests, optional advanced path. Observation note only.
70–80 Personal project or pathway work Make the session personally meaningful. Choose Guided, Build, Remix, or Stretch and work on school, planning, creativity, or a practical interest. Revision, purpose fit, checking output. Personal mini-project result.
80–87 Reflection Turn activity into learning. Record what changed, what worked, what surprised them, what gap remained, what they would verify. Hallucination, bias, overconfidence, checking. Reflection page / note.
87–90 Closing End with safe-use takeaways and confidence. Leave with one improved prompt, one thing to verify, one next-step idea. Ask clearly, check what matters, use AI to support thinking. Takeaway summary.

Detailed live-session flow

1) Arrival, reassurance, and relevance

Instructor moveParticipant moveWhy this block matters
Welcome the group. Normalize mixed skill levels. Make clear that browser-first is enough and nobody needs coding experience.Settle in, get device ready, answer the opening question.Reduces intimidation and gives the night an immediate personal anchor.

Opening learner question: Write one thing you hope AI could help you do better by the end of tonight.

2) Foundations: what AI is, what ChatGPT is, what it is not

TeachPlain-language versionKeep / simplify
AIAI tools are good at patterns, drafts, suggestions, and language tasks.Keep
Normal software vs AINormal software follows exact rules; many AI systems learn patterns from examples.Keep
Model / engineThe trained system doing the prediction work.Keep
ToolThe product people use, like ChatGPT.Keep
AgentA system that can take steps toward a goal instead of answering only once.Keep, briefly
ChatGPTA browser-based AI assistant that helps with drafting, organizing, explaining, and starting.Keep

3) How ChatGPT works

PointHow to teach it
It predicts patterns, not human truthUse the “likely next words / likely next pattern” explanation, not deep technical detail.
Prompt quality mattersShow that clearer goal + audience + format + constraints improves usefulness.
Helpful does not mean correctIntroduce checking early so learners do not equate polished output with truth.

4) Vocabulary to cover during the session

TermUse in session
PromptThe instruction or request you give the AI.
OutputThe response the AI gives back.
HallucinationWhen AI sounds confident but is wrong.
BiasNarrow or unfair patterns in answers or examples.
AccuracyWhether the answer is factually sound.
VerificationWhat should still be checked by a human.
DriftLight-touch facilitator term only: results can shift when context, wording, or conditions change. This term fits your stated intent even though it is less central in the uploaded workshop files.

5) Demo sequence

DemoPurposeBest use
Demo 1: weak text prompt vs strong text promptShow the core pattern of improvement.Main anchor demo.
Demo 2: weak image prompt vs strong image promptShow that the same thinking applies to visual generation.Creative middle block.
Demo 3: facilitator-only advanced pathShow that AI tools can do more complex multi-step work, but keep it clearly secondary.Short optional advanced/future path.

6) Learner practice system

PathwayWho it is forWhat they do
GuidedUnsure, slower-starting, or overwhelmed learnersUse narrower sentence frames and starter prompts.
BuildLearners ready for a practical taskImprove a real prompt for something useful.
RemixEarly finishers or confident learnersChange audience, tone, format, or style.
StretchStrong movers who want more challengeCompare outputs, judge fit, explain what still needs checking.

8) Reflection model

QuestionWhy it stays
What changed?Connects revision to result.
What worked?Reinforces success patterns.
What surprised you?Encourages curiosity, not just correctness.
What gap remained?Stops the session from treating AI output as final truth.
What would you verify?Builds safe-use habits.
What would you try next?Makes the learning portable beyond the session.
