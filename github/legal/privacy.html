Practical privacy notice

Minimal data, one-event purpose, and volunteer-level handling.

This notice describes the privacy model for this independent volunteer support site, the workshop readiness flow, and the parent approval process it supports.

Independent site notice: this page is not an official Scouts Canada privacy notice and does not replace any official organizational policy.

Core rule

Collect only what the event actually needs, keep access narrow, and delete it after the event follow-up window.

Purpose of collection

  • Coordinate one AI Coding Night event.
  • Confirm participant approval by a parent or guardian when needed.
  • Understand readiness for devices, ChatGPT access, and workshop support needs.
  • Share limited event-related communication.

Who uses the information

  • The volunteer organizer.
  • Leaders or facilitator who need it for event coordination or safeguarding.
  • The external form handler only if configured for submission delivery.

Minimal readiness data

  • First name
  • Device readiness
  • ChatGPT account readiness
  • Current comfort level
  • What the participant wants to build or explore

Not collected by default

  • Birthdate
  • Home address
  • Passwords
  • Sensitive personal or medical details

Retention and disposal

  • Keep approval and readiness information only through event delivery and short follow-up.
  • Review and delete no later than 30 days after the event unless a clear reason requires earlier deletion.
  • Remove exported files, old emails, and third-party form entries during cleanup.

Practical protection

  • Use password-protected volunteer devices.
  • Avoid duplicate copies where possible.
  • Limit access to people who actually need it.
  • Keep participant prompts free of personal or sensitive information.
